The training includes 32 theory lessons and 18 driving lessons. The total duration depends on the student's availability and learning speed, but on average, it takes about 3 to 6 months. If the practical exam is not passed, it can be retaken after a 14-day waiting period. It is recommended to take additional driving lessons during this time to improve your skills and better prepare for the next exam.

If you fail the theory exam, you can retake it after a 14-day waiting period. It is important to thoroughly review the theory during this time and prepare for the retake.

You must actively participate in the 32 mandatory theory lessons and make sure you understand the material. Ask questions if anything is unclear.

  1. Use study materials: Use the study materials, books, and online resources provided by the driving school to review and deepen your understanding of the material.
  2. Online exam simulations: Use online platforms and apps that offer exam simulations. These help you better understand the question style and types of questions that may appear on the exam.
  3. Form study groups: Exchange ideas with other students, form study groups, and discuss challenging topics together.
  4. Study regularly: Set aside time for daily study. Short, regular study sessions are more effective than long, irregular study intervals.
  5. Identify weak areas: Pay attention to topics you find difficult and focus particularly on these areas.
  6. Stay calm: Try to get enough sleep before the exam and approach it calmly. Stress and panic can negatively affect your performance.

The 18 driving lessons are scheduled in consultation with the driving school. You can choose dates that fit your schedule. The lessons are structured so that you gradually learn all the necessary skills, from the basics to more complex driving situations. It is recommended to evenly distribute the driving lessons over the training period to ensure continuous learning and improvement.

In general, the driving school offers a variety of vehicles. You can usually choose whether you want to learn in a manual or automatic vehicle. The availability of specific vehicle models may vary. It is best to discuss your preferences with the driving school early on.

Some driving schools offer intensive courses, where the theory and practical lessons are completed within a shorter period. Advantages of an intensive course include faster completion of the training and the opportunity to prepare intensively for the driving test in a compact time frame. Intensive courses are especially suitable for people who want to obtain their driver's license quickly and have the necessary time and focus for an intensive learning phase.

On the day of the exam, you should be well-rested and focused. Make sure you had a good night's sleep. Bring all the necessary documents, such as your ID card and registration confirmation. Arrive at the exam location on time to avoid stress and, if necessary, coordinate with your driving instructor. Before the practical exam, it is often possible to take a short refresher drive to calm down and adjust. Stay calm and focused during the exam. Mistakes can happen; the important thing is to stay calm and correct them.
